The Opportunity

Across the country, forward-thinking civic leaders like you are activating innovation in their cities. There’s energy and momentum coalescing around the potential of open government and tech for civic innovation, and it's the individuals working within municipal government who are uniquely positioned to drive forward real change.

The Code for America Peer Network is about connecting and supporting these individuals to amplify your impact -- both in your city and collectively. As part of the Peer Network, you'll get access to unprecedented opportunities to exchange ideas and collaborate on shared challenges with likeminded leaders across the country; access to a wealth of gov 2.0 resources, training, and expertise to help you develop professionally; and structured support designed to facilitate .

What is it?

A membership-based professional learning network, the CfA Peer Network is a online and in-person community that builds capacity for innovation in local government by offering networking, training, and professional development opportunities -- along with a structured program to help you drive innovation in your city.

Who Joins?

The Peer Network is for civic innovators in government from cities and counties of all sizes. We are inclusive by nature but a sampling of our membership may include:
  • C-Level City Staff: Information, Technology, Data, Innovation, etc
  • City Councilors, Aldermen
  • City Managers and Mayor’s Chiefs of Staff
  • Directors of Civic Engagement and Volunteer Services
  • Directors of Information Technology
  • Directors of Planning and Economic Development Directors
  • Other Department Directors

Want to try it out?

What is Code for America?

Founded by Jennifer Pahlka in 2009, Code for America is a non-profit that's trying to make government work more like the internet: more open, more efficient, and more collaborative. Code for America launched with the CfA Fellowship, an annual program, which pairs passionate technologists with innovative city governments to build new solutions to civic problems. (To learn more about the broader context of CfA's work, see Jen's recent TED Talk.)

The CfA Peer Network emerges from our learnings from the Fellowship and the CfA Summit that individuals within City Hall are driving forces for innovation, and their impact can be amplified when connected to and supported by other likeminded leaders around the country.
