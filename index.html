The Opportunity

Local governments are changing. Forward-thinking leaders in government across the country are leveraging technology to innovate the way our cities work — from deploying new apps for engagement to advocating for new policies and protocols that reshape City Hall. Yet innovation often happens in isolation: knowledge isn’t shared and opportunities for collaboration are missed.

It’s time we connect, support, and empower these civic leaders — and build a network of local change agents brought together by the common goal of taking innovation in their city to the next level.

That’s why we’re launching the Code for America Peer Network.

The Program

The CfA Peer Network connects change agents in local government around big ideas with the potential to make government more engaged with citizens and more effective. Through events, resources, and networking opportunities, we’ll build a network of local innovators empowered to be more effective in their cities and in their careers — and drive forward our collective vision for a new kind of public service.

The program will launch publicly in early 2013; If you're interested, let us know. →

How it Works

  • Bringing together innovators from across the country

    Leaders from cities and counties of all sizes commit to working together to have a greater impact.

  • Aligning on common goals, challenges, and priorities

    Shared focus areas identified by members inform the curriculum and content for the year.

  • Supporting collaboration through events, expert resources, and curated content

    We provide the tools, support, and structure for collaboration — so members can focus on the work that matters most.

Benefits of Participation

Connect and collaborate:
Regular working groups, special events, and facilitated online discussion lets you connect with likeminded leaders to exchange knowledge, solutions, and best practices.

Content and curriculum:
Custom content to support member-identified goals, like deployment guides for CfA-vetted apps, best practice case studies, and webinars with industry experts.

A national platform:
Showcase what’s already been done in your city and lead the way in establishing best practices that can become the new standards for innovation.

FAQ

Got questions? Good. Here are some answers, and we'll keep adding to the list over time. Still not sure about something? No problem. Drop us a line (email or @codeforamerica), and we'll get you squared away.

What is the CfA Peer Network?

It’s a membership-based, peer learning community that will mobilize innovation in cities by supporting and connecting forward-thinking local government leaders across the country. The Peer Network provides structure for collaboration and knowledge exchange between cities — supplemented by tools, information resources, professional development and training, and access to outside experts.

What is Code for America?

Founded by Jennifer Pahlka in 2009, Code for America is a non-profit that's trying to make government work more like the internet: more open, more efficient, and more collaborative. Code for America launched with the CfA Fellowship, an annual program, which pairs passionate technologists with innovative city governments to build new solutions to civic problems. (To learn more about the broader context of CfA's work, see Jen's recent TED Talk.)

Who can join the Peer Network?

Membership is open to government innovators from cities and counties of all sizes. The only requirements? You work within government, and you have a passion for civic innovation.

When can I join the Peer Network?

We’re currently in closed beta; we’ll be launching publicly in early 2013. Interested in joining? Let us know, and we’ll make sure you’re the first to know when membership is open.

I don't work in government, but I want to get involved. What can I do?

Check out the Code for America Brigade, our network of volunteer civic hackers across the country. There might be a Brigade in your community already, or you can start one today.
