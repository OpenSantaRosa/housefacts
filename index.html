The Opportunity

In cities across the country, forward-thinking civic leaders like you are mobilizing innovation within City Hall to help their government work better, cut costs, and be open and engaged with citizens.

There's a unique opportunity today to scale up this impact by connecting likeminded leaders across the country and arming them with the learnings and resources of the greater Code for America community. Introducing the Code for America Peer Network: a membership-based peer learning community that connects and supports civic innovators to help them amplify and scale innovation in their cities.

The Program

As part of the Peer Network, you'll get access to unprecedented opportunities to exchange ideas and collaborate on shared challenges with likeminded leaders across the country; access to a wealth of gov 2.0 resources, training, and expertise to help you develop professionally; and structured support designed to fit your goals for your city.

The program will kickoff in October 2012; If you're interested, let us know. →

Benefits of Participation

Connect with likeminded leaders: Regular working groups, special events, and facilitated online discussion let you connect with other leaders and collaborate around shared challenges and goals.

Tap into knowledge: Access a repository of custom content generated to support member-identified goals, including deployment guides for civic apps, best practice case studies, and policy toolkits.

Develop professionally: Monthly webinars with experts in the space, self-assessment tools, in-person and online workshops, and personalized consultation — all designed to help you take innovation in your city to the next level.

What is Code for America?

Founded by Jennifer Pahlka in 2009, Code for America is a non-profit that's trying to make government work more like the internet: more open, more efficient, and more collaborative. Code for America launched with the CfA Fellowship, an annual program, which pairs passionate technologists with innovative city governments to build new solutions to civic problems. (To learn more about the broader context of CfA's work, see Jen's recent TED Talk.)

The CfA Peer Network emerges from our learnings from the Fellowship and the CfA Summit that individuals within City Hall are driving forces for innovation, and their impact can be amplified when connected to and supported by other likeminded leaders around the country.
