Why?

Across the country, forward-thinking civic leaders like you are activating innovation in their cities. There’s unprecedented energy and momentum around the potential of tech for civic innovation that’s driving real change in our cities. And this impact can be amplified when these leaders connect, exchange ideas, and collaborate on shared problems -- we’re already seeing it happen.

The Code for America Peer Network will connect and support innovators in local government by providing them with infrastructure for collaboration, access to a wealth of gov 2.0 resources and expertise, and support to drive forward action in their cities.

What is it?

A membership-based professional learning community, the CfA Peer Network is a online and in-person community that builds capacity for innovation in local government by offering networking, trainings, and professional development -- along with a structured program to help you bring change to your city.

The program will kickoff in October 2012; If you're interested, let us know. →

Benefits of Participation

Connect with colleagues who are committed to the same mission you are.

Gain recognition for yourself and your city as a leader in civic innovation.

Take advantage of professional development and training opportunities.

Tap into the knowledge and expertise of the Code for America network.

Who Joins?

The Peer Network is a network for civic innovators in government from cities and counties of all sizes. We are inclusive by nature but a sampling of our membership may include:
  • C-Level City Staff: Information, Technology, Data, Innovation, etc
  • City Councilors, Aldermen
  • City Managers and Mayor’s Chiefs of Staff
  • Directors of Civic Engagement and Volunteer Services
  • Directors of Information Technology
  • Directors of Planning and Economic Development Directors
  • Other Department Directors

FAQ

What is the CfA Accelerator?

The CfA Accelerator is an early stage seed startup accelerator focused on the civic space – that is, government, community, and the interaction between the two. Increasingly the way in which we engage with government is channeled through technology, which means there's a tremendous opportunity for entrepreneurs to build new, successful, and profitable startups that make the experience more like the consumer web. The CfA Accelerator aims to help early-stage civic startups get the financial, strategic, and operational support they need to succeed.

What is Code for America?

Founded by Jennifer Pahlka in 2009, Code for America is a non-profit that's trying to make government work more like the internet: more open, more efficient, and more collaborative. Code for America launched with the CfA Fellowship, an annual program, which pairs passionate technologists with innovative city governments to build new solutions to civic problems. (To learn more about the broader context of CfA's work, see Jen's recent TED Talk.)

The CfA Accelerator is an outgrowth of our learnings from the fellowship; primarily a realization that while change from the inside of government is necessary, it must come from the outside as well.

What's a civic startup?

Good question. We see three models for startups that'd fall within the civic space: 1) they provide services on top of open government data; 2) they bring modern web technologies directly to governments; 3) they change the way citizens ask, get, or need services from government.
